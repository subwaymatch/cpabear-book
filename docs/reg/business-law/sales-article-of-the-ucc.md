# Sales Article of the UCC

## Overview

The **Sales Article of the UCC (Uniform Commercial Code)** is a set of laws that governs the sale of goods in the U.S. It standardizes rules for contracts involving the buying and selling of tangible, movable goods to make commercial transactions smoother and more predictable.

**Key points about the Sales Article:**

- Applies to transactions involving **goods** (not services or real estate).
- Covers contract formation, performance, breach, and remedies.
- Includes rules on warranties, risk of loss, and delivery terms.

## Warranty of Title

The **warranty of title** under the Sales Article is an **implied warranty** that automatically arises in every sale of goods unless specifically disclaimed. It means:

- The seller guarantees they have **good legal title** to the goods.
- The goods are **free from any undisclosed liens, claims, or encumbrances**.
- The buyer will receive ownership **free of any hidden legal claims** they didn't know about at the time of sale.

> This warranty protects the buyer from surprises like the goods being subject to a creditor's lien or another party's claim.
