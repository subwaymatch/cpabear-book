# Supplementary Information

## Required Supplementary Information (RSI)

RSI is information that a designated accounting standard-setter (like the FASB or GASB) mandates must accompany the basic financial statements.

**Example:** Management's Discussion and Analysis (MD&A) in a corporate report, or required schedules for governmental entities.

### Auditor's Responsibility

The auditor must apply **limited procedures** to the RSI. This means:

- Asking management how the information was prepared
- Checking it for consistency with the basic financial statements

### Level of Assurance

The auditor does **not** audit the RSI and does **not** express an opinion on it.

### Reporting

The auditor adds an **Other-Matter paragraph** (for nonissuers) or an **Explanatory paragraph** (for issuers) to the audit report. This paragraph states that:

1. The RSI is included
2. Limited procedures were applied
3. An opinion on the RSI is explicitly disclaimed

---

## Other (Voluntary) Supplementary Information

This is information that management voluntarily chooses to include alongside the audited financial statements to provide more detail.

**Example:** Detailed schedules of operating expenses, consolidating schedules, or historical summaries.

### Auditor's Responsibility

The auditor's responsibility depends on what they are engaged to do.

#### Scenario A — Engaged to Report on the SI

The auditor performs specific audit procedures on the supplementary information to determine if it is **fairly stated in all material respects in relation to the financial statements as a whole**. This requires that the auditor:

- Has also audited the basic financial statements, **and**
- Did not issue an adverse opinion or a disclaimer of opinion

#### Scenario B — Not Engaged to Report on the SI

If the information is included in a document containing the audited financial statements (often referred to as "Other Information"), the auditor is only required to **read it** to ensure there are no material inconsistencies with the audited financial statements.

### Reporting (if Engaged to Report)

The auditor expresses an opinion on whether the supplementary information is **fairly stated in relation to the financial statements as a whole**. This opinion can be:

- Included in an **Other-Matter paragraph** in the main auditor's report, or
- Issued as a **separate report**

---

## Key Takeaways for the AUD Exam

| Type | Procedures | Opinion |
|------|-----------|---------|
| RSI | Limited procedures | No opinion (disclaimed) |
| Voluntary SI (when engaged) | Audit procedures | Opinion "in relation to" the financial statements |

- **RSI = Limited Procedures + No Opinion.** You just check to make sure it's there and makes sense.
- **Voluntary SI (when engaged) = Audit Procedures + Opinion "in relation to" the financial statements.**
- Always look for the phrase **"in relation to the financial statements as a whole."** This is the specific level of assurance given when an auditor is engaged to report on supplementary information.
